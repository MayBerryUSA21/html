Why Downtown Dallas?

Downtown Dallas has a vibrant history. Downtown Dallas is where Jack Ruby, and Benny Binion ran night clubs,
strip clubs, and dance halls. Benny Binion was a convicted killer, Jack Ruby killed Lee Harvey Oswald. Illegal gambling,
narcotics, and prostitution operation in Dallas with the support of local politicians.

Dallas red-light district (Boggy Bayou) located between Commerce Street and the present-day Convention Center.
City of Dallas is also where Doc Holliday became a professional gambler after his dental practice failed which located
Elm Street, east of Market Street. Dallas was once home to more than 100 professional gamblers. Dr. W.W. Samuell bequest 320 acres of land,
and $1.2 milion ($18 million into days standards) to Dallas to create parks. City of Dallas has one of the richest park system in the country.

Everyone knows about the Shootout at the O.K. Corral that lasted only 30 seconds. Very few knows about the three-day gunfight in downtown
Dallas between gamblers in a gambling house stand-off against the city mayor.

Downtown Dallas is also where Ray Charles became a professional singer, at 508 Park Ave. many young blues singers recorded famous session.
Blind Lemon Jefferson, Huddie William "Lead Belly" Ledbetter just a few that recorded their blues at 508 Park Ave.

Young T-Bone Walker, and Blind Lemon danced for spare change along Central Tracks in the Deep Ellum Blues area.
North Dallas, Deep Ellum, Stringtown where Freeman segregated towns. African-Americans built shops,the Harlem theaters, and Gypsy Tea Room
which became the heart of Dallas culture heritage. By 1940's Dallas Elm Street had more movie theaters than any other city in the United States.
East of the West End the Frogtown housed over 400 prostitutes. The buildings in Frogtown owned by Dallas leading citizens.
